Tampons

What are they?

Tampons are feminine products that are designed to absord menstrual blood by inserting it in the vagina.
They are made of cotton and rayon. They decrease the chances of leakage compared to period pads.

Are there any risks?

According to the FDA, the level of dioxins are low enough to be considered safe.
However, the fact remains that it is a very dangerous chemical and a by-product of bleaching.
Even if it is considered safe, what about the thousands of tampons that women use in their life time?
Even if one tampon may not cause harm, the build up of the exposure to these chemicals can build up to harm the body.

Also, if you leave a tampon in for too long,
it can create a breeding ground for bacteria and increase the risk of yeast infections, bacterial vaginosis or the life-threatening toxic shock syndrome.
